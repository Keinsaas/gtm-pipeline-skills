# Persona: RevOps Manager / Sales Ops Lead

> Example persona doc for Flowline (fictional). Use as a reference for structuring your own persona files.

---

## Who They Are

The RevOps Manager owns the revenue tech stack and is responsible for making the sales process run smoothly. They sit between sales leadership (who want more pipeline) and engineering (who don't prioritize internal tools). They're usually the person manually fixing broken handoffs or building Zapier automations at 6pm.

**Typical titles:** Revenue Operations Manager, Sales Operations Manager, GTM Operations Lead, Sales Ops Specialist (Senior)

**Company size:** 50–300 employees. Below this, the COO or a sales manager does this job. Above 300, it's usually a team.

**Seniority:** IC to manager. Rarely director or above at this size.

---

## Their Day-to-Day Pain

- CRM data is always out of date because reps don't update it
- Lead routing is manual or depends on a spreadsheet that's always broken
- Approvals (discounts, contracts, exceptions) happen via Slack DMs — no audit trail
- They built 20 Zaps but they keep breaking and nobody knows why
- Engineering won't prioritize internal tooling — they're "not revenue-generating"
- Every time a new tool gets added, they have to rebuild integrations from scratch

---

## What Resonates in Outreach

- Specific pain points (not "streamline your workflows")
- Reference to tools they likely use (HubSpot, Salesforce, Jira, Slack)
- Proof that it works without engineering ("you deploy it, not your dev team")
- Time-to-value metrics ("most customers automate their first flow in under a day")

## What Falls Flat

- Generic "AI-powered automation" language
- Enterprise case studies (they don't relate)
- ROI calculators (they don't trust them without proof)
- Anything that sounds like more work to maintain

---

## Outreach Hook Ideas

- Reference a specific tool in their stack (if known from signal data)
- Mention a recent ops hire or CRM migration (if signal exists)
- Acknowledge the engineering bottleneck directly
- Lead with time savings, not capability

---

## Example Message Structure

```
Hook: Specific signal or shared pain point — no fluff greeting
Bridge: "We work with [similar company type] who had the same problem"
Offer: One sentence on what we do and how it's different
CTA: Low-commitment ask ("worth a 20-min call?")
```

**Character target:** 320–420 characters (LinkedIn connection request limit)
