## What They Sell

Flowline is a B2B workflow automation platform for operations and revenue teams. It connects internal tools (CRM, ERP, project management) and automates repetitive handoff processes — approvals, data sync, status updates, notifications — without requiring engineering resources.

The core product is a no-code workflow builder with pre-built connectors for Salesforce, HubSpot, SAP, Jira, Slack, and 80+ other tools.

## Target Buyer

**Primary:** Operations managers, RevOps leads, and heads of sales operations at B2B companies with 50–500 employees. These buyers own the tool stack, experience the pain of manual processes, and have budget authority for SaaS tools in the $1k–5k/month range.

**Secondary:** VPs of Sales and COOs at smaller companies (< 100 employees) where the ops function doesn't have a dedicated owner.

## Value Proposition

> We automate the manual handoffs that slow your revenue team down — no engineering required.

Flowline customers typically reclaim 6–10 hours per week per ops person in the first month. The most common use case is automating CRM data entry, lead routing, and approval workflows that were previously handled via Slack or email chains.

## Reference Customers / Key Segments

- Mid-market B2B SaaS companies (Series A–C) scaling their sales motion
- B2B services firms (consulting, staffing, agencies) managing high-volume pipelines
- Distributors and wholesale companies modernizing legacy processes
- Companies that recently hired a Head of RevOps or Sales Ops (growth signal)

## Anti-ICP

- Pure software or developer-tooling companies (build internally)
- Enterprise (> 1,000 employees) — long cycles, procurement complexity
- Consumer-facing businesses without a B2B sales motion
- Healthcare and finance with strict compliance requirements (not yet certified)

## Tone of Voice

Conversational, direct, and outcome-focused. Not corporate. References real problems ("your reps are still manually copying data from HubSpot to SAP") rather than buzzwords ("we leverage AI to synergize your workflows").

Outreach language: casual but professional. Uses specific numbers and named tools when possible. Avoids "I hope this message finds you well."
